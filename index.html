What we treat

Our aligners are engineered to correct a wide range of clinical cases with precision and comfort. No matter your smile's starting point, we have a path for you.

01

Crowded Teeth

When there's not enough room in the jaw for all teeth to fit normally, leading to overlap and misalignment.

02

Gapped Teeth

Extra space between two or more teeth, often caused by jaw growth or missing teeth.

03

Overbite

When upper front teeth significantly overlap lower front teeth, which can cause wear and jaw pain.

04

Underbite

When lower front teeth protrude past upper front teeth, affecting facial profile and chewing function.

05

Crossbite

When some upper teeth sit inside the lower teeth when biting, often leading to tooth wear.

06

Open Bite

When upper and lower teeth don't touch when the mouth is closed, affecting speech and eating.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about your AlignPro transformation journey.

How long does the treatment typically take?

Most AlignPro treatments are completed within 6 to 12 months. However, the exact duration depends on the complexity of your case. You'll see your estimated timeline during your 3D simulation phase.

Is wearing aligners painful?

You may feel a slight pressure for the first day or two after switching to a new set of aligners. This is a good sign—it means your teeth are moving into their new positions with clinical precision.

How many hours a day should I wear them?

For optimal results, you should wear your aligners for 20 to 22 hours a day. They should only be removed for eating, drinking (anything other than water), and your daily oral hygiene routine.

Can I eat and drink while wearing aligners?

We recommend removing your aligners before eating or drinking anything except plain water. This prevents staining of the material and avoids trapping sugar or food particles against your teeth.

How do I clean my AlignPro trays?

The best way to clean your aligners is to rinse them in lukewarm water and gently brush them with a soft-bristled toothbrush and mild soap. Avoid hot water, as it can warp the medical-grade polymer.
